Thinking about these quotes after the insanity of the past four years. Wish artists had anywhere else to go but I guess we're all stuck here forever. Definitely feels like we're part of the problem though.

After the fog of the last four years lifts it seems clear that social media platforms played an immeasurable role in this unforgivable insanity that has resulted in so much pain, confusion and death. According to many in the tech industry, the world would be a safer and healthier place if we all left big social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter and Instagram. But visual artists have nowhere else to go, so most of us have no choice but to stay. It feels more and more like we're dumping our hearts and souls into a platform that wasn't made for us, isn't good for us and that we're all just contributing to the problem that almost destroyed us in 2016.

Short-ish Version

I've been watching instagram erode art communities, professional relationships and the critical viability of studio practices for the better part of a decade. During that time I've also seen a small number of colleagues get tangible rewards from it like exhibition oportounities and sales that I don't believe they would have found without instagram.

I finished grad school when instagram was still just an app to post photos of your dogs, meals and vacations on. My perspective is also shaped by my experience as both a practicing fine artist and a working software engineer.

Social media is bad for artists in the same way it's bad for everybody else (duh), except it's more than just cheap entertainment that's at stake for us. Unlike musicians or small businesses who have a viable ecosystem of other platforms, visual artists are increasingly stuck with only the option of instagram, which it's worth remembering wasn't designed for visual artists and whose layout and social algorithms often directly inhibit our needs. With the increased deterioration of physical arts institutions, if we're not on instagram we simply don't exist, so we're not going anywhere.



Who is Jaron Lanier?

In short, he is the quintessential tech hippie and an accomplished software engineer specializing in virtual reality. He's written several books that attempt to reach outside of the software engineering community to the software consuming community that increasingly grows to encompass the totality of our species.

I've been watching instagram erode my professional connections since I got out of grad school. It's coerced us into shallow ego-driven behavior that I don't think reflects our charactors and it has alienated us into an endless petty competition that none of us will ever win. In response to this I decided to read through Jaron Lanier's 2018 book "10 Reasons You Should Delete Your Social Media Accounts Right Now".

I recently switched from adjuncting painting, drawing and art history to software engineering in the hopes of better supporting my studio practice. Their culture‚Äìand sometimes refreshing absence of culture‚Äìhas fascinated me, and although Lanier is a somewhat polarizing figure, few engineers working in tech disagree with his conclusions whole-heartedly. The perspective he outlines in his books and interviews seems to reflect the general foundation of assumptions that many engineers seem to operate under, even if they don't in any way embody his charactor.

In any case it's a fun empowering project to take on!



The Good

Our community's exodus onto instagram has had good and bad consequences. It connects us in a common place, but in my experience at least this was also accomplished by myspace and facebook, with deviantart maybe being a notable player as well.

Not a Conclusion

There is no conclusion. There aren't any viable alternatives to Instagram and the physical art world is falling apart. We're all stuck here and our only choice is to get with the program and make work specifically tuned to get preference from the feed algorithms or stick to our guns and be proud of the 14 likes we get on works that we pour our heart and soul and hundreds of hours into.
